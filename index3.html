«Английский за минуту» - Walk on Eggshells – Ходить на цыпочках, быть предельно деликатным
  • Welcome to English in a Minute.
  • The hard shell around an egg is thin and delicate.
  • Walk on Eggshells.
  • How is your roommate doing?
  • Not well. She's still pretty upset about her breakup. These days, I stay out of her way. I am very quiet. I don't ask her to clean, or...pay rent.
  • Wow. Talk about walking on eggshells.
  • Seriously. It seems like no matter what I say, she starts crying.
  • An eggshell is delicate. And so is a situation where you must walk on them.
  • You try hard not to anger or upset someone.
  • You have to think about what to say and how to act.
  • If you walk on eggshells around someone all the time, it may not be a very healthy relationship.
  • And that's English in a Minute.
«Английский за минуту» - Lock Horns - Сцепиться, бодаться
  • Welcome to English in a Minute.
  • Many animals have horns.
  • They use them to fight other animals.
  • LOCK HORNS.
  • We shouldn't use red letters in our proposal. Red says "wrong!" or "emergency! Emergency!"
  • We should definitely use red, Anna. It catches the eye!
  • Look, let's not lock horns over these little things.
  • You're right. When the project really starts, we'll have much bigger things to fight over!
  • Imagine two large bull fighting. They strike each other. Then their horns lock. To lock horns with someone means to fight or argue about something. You can "lock horns" in many situations, and it usually is not very pleasant or fun.
  • And that's English in a Minute.

  • Welcome to English in a Minute!

  • On its Last Legs...

«Английский за минуту» - Run-of-the-Mill - Заурядный
  • Welcome to English in a Minute!
  • A mill is a factory where machines are used to make things.
  • Run-of-the-Mill
  • Jonathan, did you ever eat at that restaurant we talked about last week?
  • Yeah, it was okay. It turned out to be just a run-of-the-mill hamburger shop.
  • Really? that's too bad. The giant hamburger they had on the sign made it look like it would be special.
  • So special.
  • The expression "run-of-the-mill" means that something is ordinary. It has no special features or characteristics. Some experts say the mill in the expression refers to a weaving mill, where clothes were made in the early days of the United States. The articles of clothing were called "run of the mill." And that's English in a Minute.
