    «Газетная лексика» - Vulnerable - Уязвимый
    • Welcome to the Voice of America's News Words.
    • Last spring, as immigrant families arrived at the U.S. southern border, officials separated children from their parents.
    • VULNERABLE
    • The Trump administration's policy called for the arrest of migrants who came into the U.S. illegally. Families were separated during legal proceedings. Doctors say young children are the most vulnerable to health problems from family separations.
    • If people are vulnerable, they can be easily hurt physically, mentally or emotionally.
    • Things like wounds or places are vulnerable when they are open to harm, attack or damage.
    «Газетная лексика» - Stigma - Стереотип
    • Welcome to the Voice of America's News Words.
    • A project called Bicicletear supports the use of bicycles for fun.
    • STIGMA
    • In the early 1990s, thousands of bicycles were given to Cubans after the fall of the Soviet Union. It was a time of economic hardship. Now, dozens of cyclists ride 20 kilometers through Havana once a month. The idea is to lose the stigma that bicycles still have with many Cubans.
    • A stigma is a bad and often unfair belief that a society or a group of people have about something.
    • It causes a loss of honor or respect for someone or something.
    «Газетная лексика» - Dire - Катастрофический
    • Welcome to the Voice of America's News Words.
    • The United Nations Refugee Agency reports there are now more than 68 million refugees around the world.
    • DIRE
    • Chris Boian is with the UN.
    • He says the international refugee situation is dire.
    • There's a person forcibly displaced now every two seconds. These are people that are fleeing not by choice but because they have no choice but to flee if they want to stay alive.
    • A situation that is dire requires immediate action because it is serious or extreme and difficult to solve.
    • A dire warning signals a very bad future that can cause worry or great fear.
    «Газетная лексика» - Remittance - Денежный перевод
    • Welcome to the Voice of America's News Words.
    • The World Bank says migrant workers in the United States have increased the amount of money they send home.
    • REMITTANCE
    • Last year, $466 billion in remittances flowed to low and middle-income countries. That figure is estimated to grow to $485 billion this year.
    • Remittances are a lifeline to many countries that are facing some sort of a crisis. The poorer the country, the larger their dependence on remittances.
    • A remittance is money sent, usually by mail, as a payment or as a gift.
    • Here, it is money that migrants earn in one country, and then send to their families in their home countries.

    • In sports, a team that does not follow the rules may have to forfeit - or lose - the game.

    «Газетная лексика» – Anonymous – Анонимный
    • Welcome to the Voice of America's News Words.
    • In September, The New York Times newspaper published an opinion criticizing U.S. President Donald Trump.
    • ANONYMOUS
    • The anonymous opinion was written by a senior official in Trump's government.
    • "When you tell me about some anonymous source within the administration, probably who's failing and probably here for all the wrong reasons..."
    • Someone who is anonymous is not identified by name. This opinion was unusual because major newspapers in the U.S. usually require writers to sign their work.
    • When an author does not want to be known, publishers call him or her "anonymous."
    «Газетная лексика» – Superfood – Суперпродукты
    • Welcome to the Voice of America's News Words.
    • The Baobab is a well-known tree in Africa.
    • SUPERFOOD
    • Inside the green shell that comes from the tree is a tasty fruit. For farmers in Senegal, the business of selling fruit from the baobab tree has increased in the last year. The fruit has become a popular superfood in the United States and Europe.
    • A superfood is something to eat that has a lot of nutrients and vitamins. Superfoods improve health and well-being.
    • They include salmon, sardines, spinach, broccoli, baobab and blueberries.

    «Газетная лексика» – Incubator – Инкубатор
    • Welcome to the Voice of America's News Words.
    • Washing your hands is one of the easiest ways to stop the spread of disease.
    • INCUBATOR
    • Two women created a fun way for children to wash their hands. It is a cleaner drawn on the skin and removed by washing. The women did not have much business experience, so they turned to a business incubator for help in selling the product.
    • A business incubator is an organization that helps young companies grow and develop. It offers training, technical support, and other services.
    • More commonly, an incubator is a device that keeps eggs or even new babies warm.
